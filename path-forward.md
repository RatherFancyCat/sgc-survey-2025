# The Path Forward for the SGC
<div class="alert alert-success" markdown="1">
In this portion, we will discuss the agreed upon choices made by our Staff in regards to the feedback given.
</div>

## Controlled Multi-Game Expansion

<div class="alert alert-warning" markdown="1">
One of the topics that came out of the leadership discussion was how the SGC approaches games outside of Destiny.

First and most importantly, Destiny will remain the core identity of the SGC and our clans for the foreseeable future. The community was built around Destiny, our clan structure exists around Destiny, and that is not changing anytime soon.

That said, we also recognize that people enjoy playing other games, especially during slower periods in Destiny. Because of that, we will continue expanding support for other games inside the SGC. This will be optional engagement, not a full pivot away from Destiny.

The goal is simple: give members more things to do with SGC friends without losing the core identity of the community.
</div>

## Multi-Game Support Structure

<div class="alert alert-warning" markdown="1">
To support this, we will continue maintaining multi-game areas within the Discord and expand them when appropriate.

This includes things like:

• Creating channels for other popular games
• Creating clans in those games when enough players are interested
• Encouraging activity in those spaces when they gain traction
• Adding optional bounties that can earn Loyalty Points or other rewards

These areas will exist to give members additional ways to play together without affecting the Destiny clan structure.
</div>


## Rotating Game Support

<div class="alert alert-warning" markdown="1">
We will periodically review which non-Destiny games the community is actively playing.

If certain game sections become inactive, they may be rotated out and replaced with games that are currently trending within the community.

This review will likely happen on a quarterly basis, though adjustments may happen sooner if activity shifts quickly.
</div>

## How We Will Track Activity

<div class="alert alert-warning" markdown="1">

To determine which games are worth supporting, we will use several sources of information.

First, we will occasionally ask clan staff and division leaders to informally check in with their clans about what games people are playing outside of Destiny.

You may occasionally see your clan leadership ask questions about what other games are popular within your group.

Second, we will use StatBot data. StatBot tracks the games our members are actively playing and provides a general snapshot of what games are trending within the community.

Finally, we may run community polls inside Discord asking members which games they are currently playing or interested in.

Between these three sources, we should have a good understanding of where the community's interests are.

</div>


## Bounties and Community Player of the Week Support

<div class="alert alert-warning" markdown="1">

One area we are currently exploring is expanding Community Player of the Week support into multi-game activities.

Right now, most Community Player of the Week activity is tied to Destiny.

We are looking into ways for members to earn Community Player of the Week points through bounties and activities outside of Destiny as well.

This would allow members to contribute toward Community Player of the Week without needing to play Destiny every week.

This is something we are actively working on and we are excited about the potential it brings.

</div>


## Event Support for Community Leaders

<div class="alert alert-warning" markdown="1">
Another major addition is a new system that allows clan staff and community leaders to run their own events.

If you are a leader in the SGC and have an idea for an event, whether it is in Destiny or another game, you will be able to submit it through a form.

From there, leadership can review the event and potentially support it.
  
Support may include things such as:

• Help organizing the event  
• Marketing and announcements within the community  
• Loyalty Point rewards  
• Community Player of the Week points  
• Additional prize support when available

We have also created a reward matrix that helps determine what kinds of rewards can be given depending on the size and scope of the event.

This system is designed to empower leaders to create more activities across the community without needing everything to come directly from the top.

</div>


## Community Events Outside of Destiny

<div class="alert alert-warning" markdown="1">

In addition to supporting events from leaders, the SGC will occasionally run community-wide events outside of Destiny.

We have done this in the past and will continue doing it from time to time.

These events are meant to give the community something different to rally around and provide additional ways for members to spend time together.

</div>

## Cross-Clan Culture

<div class="alert alert-warning" markdown="1">

One of the strengths of the SGC has always been our clan identities. Each clan has its own personality, culture, and group of players that regularly spend time together. That identity is something we value and will not be removing or restructuring.

However, one theme that came out of both the survey and leadership discussions was the need for more positive interaction between clans.

In the past, certain competitive formats — most notably Clan Wars — created tension between clans. The issue was not just the format itself, but also the situations and discussions that sometimes surrounded it. 
Because of that, we have decided that Clan Wars will not be returning in its previous format.


Instead, we will be focusing more on cooperative or mixed-group activities that allow players from different clans to participate together.

</div>


## Cooperative Events

<div class="alert alert-warning" markdown="1">

Moving forward, we want to emphasize events that encourage players to interact across clan lines.

These may include:

• Individual competition events  
• Mixed-team community events  
• Cooperative community activities

A current example of this type of event is Zero to Hero, which has been extremely popular so far. Events like this allow members from multiple clans to participate together while still maintaining their clan identities.

We will continue exploring and developing events with this type of structure moving forward.

</div>


## Cross-Clan Event Participation

<div class="alert alert-warning" markdown="1">

Another step we are taking is encouraging events that allow participation from multiple clans.

As mentioned in other sections, we are introducing a system that allows clan staff and community leaders to run their own events with support from the SGC. When those events are opened up to players from other clans, they will receive additional support and encouragement.

The goal is to create more opportunities for players to interact and build relationships outside of their immediate clan group.

</div>


## Public Clan Voice Channels

<div class="alert alert-warning" markdown="1">
We are also encouraging clans to consider creating at least one public-facing voice chat channel.

This would allow members from other clans to join and socialize more easily without needing to permanently move between clan spaces.

This is completely optional and will be up to each clan’s leadership to decide. No clan will be required to do this.

However, we believe it can be a simple way to encourage more casual cross-clan interaction without forcing any structural changes to the community.
</div>


## Community Player of the Week Adjustments

<div class="alert alert-warning" markdown="1">

We are also making adjustments to Community Player of the Week scoring to encourage playing with members from other clans.

The goal is to reward activity that helps strengthen the overall community rather than only rewarding activity within a single clan group.

</div>

## Leadership Standards & Community Moderation

<div class="alert alert-warning" markdown="1">

Another topic that came out of the community survey and our leadership discussions was how moderation and leadership interaction happens inside the community.

Some of the feedback we received centered around the perception that higher leadership would occasionally step directly into clan chats to resolve issues. In many cases this happened because someone specifically asked us to intervene, but we recognize that the end result sometimes looked like higher leadership simply appearing in chats to enforce authority.

Since reviewing the survey results last November, we have already begun adjusting how we approach these situations.

Our goal moving forward is simple: allow clan leadership to moderate their own spaces whenever possible while ensuring there is still a clear path for escalation when needed.

</div>
</div>


## Moderation Flow

<div class="alert alert-warning" markdown="1">

For issues happening inside clan channels:

• Clan Staff (Admins / Officers) should be the first point of contact  
• If necessary, issues may escalate to the Division Leader  
• If further escalation is required, Shrouded Admins may become involved  

For issues happening in public channels:

• Moderators should be the first point of contact  
• If necessary, issues may escalate to Shrouded Admins  

Whenever possible, leadership will follow this structure before stepping in directly.

</div>


## Reverse Escalation

<div class="alert alert-warning" markdown="1">

Another important change is how we handle situations when someone goes directly to higher leadership first.

If a concern is brought directly to a Shrouded Admin, we will typically route the issue back down through the leadership structure before taking action. This means checking with Division Leaders and Clan Staff first so they have the opportunity to handle the situation themselves.

The goal is to respect the leadership structure that already exists within each clan.

There may still be rare cases where immediate action is required. In those situations leadership may step in quickly and address the situation first, then follow up afterward. These cases should be the exception rather than the norm.

</div>


## Public Callouts

<div class="alert alert-warning" markdown="1">

Another area we are actively improving is how issues are addressed publicly.

In the past, there have been situations where members were called out directly in public chats, clan chats, or even discussed during streams or podcasts. Even when intentions were good, this can easily come across as belittling or embarrassing to the person involved.

Moving forward, leadership and clan staff will make every effort to handle issues privately whenever possible.

If something begins escalating in a public conversation, the goal will be to move the discussion to a private conversation rather than continuing it publicly.

This is something leadership will continue working on, and we encourage clan staff to follow the same approach.
</div>


## Moderator Team Expansion

<div class="alert alert-warning" markdown="1">

Another improvement coming from these discussions is expanding the moderation team.

Moderators primarily focus on public channels within the community. Their role is to help resolve issues quickly without requiring higher leadership to step in for every situation.

Our plan includes:

• Expanding the moderator team so someone is available more consistently  
• Establishing a Head Moderator to help manage the moderation team  
• Ensuring moderation activity is documented and reviewed by leadership  

Moderators may step into clan chats if clan staff are unavailable, but they are expected to respect the authority of clan leadership in those spaces.

The goal is to support clan leadership, not replace it.

</div>


## Pinging Leadership

<div class="alert alert-warning" markdown="1">
Another issue that occasionally pulls leadership into situations too quickly is mass pinging.

Members sometimes ping moderators and admins simultaneously when a problem occurs. Moving forward, moderators should generally be the first contact point for issues in public channels.

Clan-related concerns should go to clan staff first.

Following this structure will help prevent situations where leadership is pulled into issues before the appropriate people have had a chance to address them.
</div>


## Favoritism and Perception

<div class="alert alert-warning" markdown="1">

This is something we take seriously, and it is also something we want to address honestly.

The reality of a gaming community is that friendships naturally form over time. Staff members run activities with certain players regularly, clan members play together often, and people get to know each other well. 

Because of that, interactions with familiar members can sometimes look different than interactions with someone we have never played with before.

That part is unavoidable in any long-term community.

However, what matters most is that rules and moderation decisions are applied consistently regardless of who is involved.

Leadership will continue working to reduce both the reality and the perception of favoritism by being mindful in how situations are handled.

One guideline we are reinforcing is that if a staff member is personally involved in a situation or closely connected to the people involved, they should step back and allow another leader to handle it.

This helps ensure moderation decisions remain fair and removes situations where personal relationships could influence outcomes.

The goal is not to eliminate friendships within the community, but to make sure that those relationships do not affect how rules are enforced.

One step we already took last year to address favoritism concerns was expanding the size of the Ban Council.

The goal of increasing the number of people involved in those decisions was to ensure that no single person or small group had too much influence over outcomes. By having more perspectives involved in reviewing 
serious enforcement decisions, it helps reduce the chance that personal relationships or biases could impact the final result.

This change was implemented specifically to create a more balanced and transparent decision process.

We recognize that favoritism perception is not limited to one area of the community, but expanding the Ban Council was an important step in addressing it where it matters most. Leadership will continue looking for ways to reduce both the reality and the perception of favoritism across other areas of the community as well.

</div>


## Conflict of Interest

<div class="alert alert-warning" markdown="1">
Finally, one leadership guideline we are reinforcing is stepping back when personally involved in a situation.

If a staff member is emotionally involved in a dispute or directly connected to the people involved, the expectation is to step back and allow another leader to handle the situation.

This helps ensure fairness and reduces the perception of bias in moderation decisions.

</div>

## Final Summary

<div class="alert alert-success" markdown="1">

Destiny will remain the foundation and core identity of the SGC. Our clans, community culture, and history are built around Destiny, and that is not changing.

At the same time, we recognize that members enjoy playing a variety of games together. Because of that, we will continue building optional multi-game opportunities within the community. This includes rotating game support, multi-game bounties, Community Player of the Week opportunities outside of Destiny, leader-supported events, and occasional community-wide activities in other games.


Clan identity will also remain a core part of the SGC. Each clan has its own culture and group of players, and that structure will continue to be preserved. Alongside that identity, we want to create more opportunities for members from different clans to interact, play together, and build stronger connections across the wider community. Cooperative events, mixed-team activities, cross-clan participation, and optional shared spaces are all part of that effort.


Finally, we are continuing to refine how leadership and moderation operate within the community. Our goal is to ensure that issues are handled at the appropriate level first while still maintaining clear escalation paths when needed. Clan leaders should feel empowered to manage their own spaces, moderators will focus on public channel moderation, and higher leadership will continue to support the community when escalation is necessary.


Many of these adjustments have already been improving how situations are handled over the past several months. We will continue refining these systems as the community grows.
The overall goal of these changes is simple: preserve what makes the SGC strong while creating more opportunities for members to stay active, connected, and engaged across the entire community.

</div>
